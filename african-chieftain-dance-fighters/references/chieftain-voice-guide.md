# Chieftain & Guard Voice Guide (Rhythmic, Meme-Safe)

Write dialogue and captions so they **read** with ceremonial boom and drum-call rhythm. Regal, clear, affectionate — not mockery of African peoples or accents.

---

## Core Voice Patterns

| Role | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Chief call | Short command, rising energy | "Guards! *Hold the line!*" |
| Guard response | Echo + commitment | "*Hai!* We hold!" |
| Crowd/drum | Onomatopoeia beat | *[Djembe: DOOM-doom-DOOM]* |
| Praise | Formal uplift | "The ancestors smile upon this footwork." |
| Wrong-place deadpan | Regal under absurdity | "The library requested silence. We requested *ceremony*." |

---

## Call-and-Response Markers (sprinkle 2–4 per meme)

`Ayeee!`, `Hai!`, `Ehe!`, `Ohoo!`, `Yawa!`, `Ashe!`, `Harambee!` (use as hype — not claiming specific ritual)

**Chief openers:** `Hear me!`, `Guards!`, `People of the floor!`, `By the spear!`

**Guard closers:** `So it is done.`, `The rhythm agrees.`, `Chief, we have stomped.`

---

## Sentence Rhythm

- Short clauses. Drum between thoughts.
- Present tense commitment: *We dance. We do not apologize.*
- No broken-English-as-punchline — voice is **oratorical**, not ESL joke
- Chief never uses slang that undercuts dignity
- Stage directions: *(spear salute)*, *(stomp line sync)*, *(Chief raises hand — silence fails)*

---

## Power Words (regal, not stereotype)

`ceremony`, `ancestors`, `rhythm`, `guard`, `honour`, `line`, `spear`, `drum`, `formation`, `royal`, `umoja` (unity), `jubilation`

**Avoid:** `tribal` (as exotic label), `primitive`, `savage`, `jungle`, `witch doctor`, mock pidgin

## Chief Jabari — Personality Notes

- Deep voice implied in ALL CAPS sparingly
- Never panics — only *reframes* chaos as tradition
- Compliments good footwork like statecraft
- Treats mundane objects (shopping cart, parking cone) as ceremonial artifacts when funny

## Stylistic Flavors (moves only — not ethnic labels)

| Flavor | Energy |
|--------|--------|
| **Jumping guard** | Vertical hops, chest held high, competitive height |
| **Stomp battalion** | Synced ground strikes, line formations |
| **Spear-salute circle** | Radial formation, spear points inward/outward on beat |
| **Shield wall shuffle** | Side-step as unit, shields clack on rhythm |

User picks flavor; do not attach flavor to a real country unless user names a setting for the *scene* (e.g. "Nairobi airport" as location, not as "all of East Africa").
