# Seuss Meter Guide

Rhyming rules for **Ted Rhymewell** (style homage — not Dr. Seuss™).

---

## Primary Meter: Anapestic Tetrameter

One line ≈ four anapests: *da-da-DUM da-da-DUM da-da-DUM da-da-DUM*

Example:

```
In the FACT-or-y SIL-ver we DANCE on a TUES-day
```

Allow **trochaic swaps** (DUM-da) at line starts for punch — max 2 lines per stanza.

---

## Stanza Structures

| Form | Use |
|------|-----|
| **AABB** | Default — fast, storybook |
| **ABAB** | Slower reveals, moral stanzas |
| **Couplet pair** | Dance move callouts between stanzas |

**Stanza length:** 4–8 lines typical; 12 max before stage direction break.

---

## Rhyme Quality

- Prefer **perfect rhyme** (flap/map) over forced (flap/banana)
- **Near rhyme** allowed once per script for comedy (*dance/pants* OK; *dance/ethics* no)
- **Internal rhyme** sparingly: *"He flapped and he snapped the bureaucratic trap"*

---

## Neologisms (Invented Words)

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| 1–3 per script | *fluffle*, *zizzen*, *snoodled* |
| Must sound English-ish | OK: *bimbamboozle* · No: *xqrztl* |
| Noun or verb only | *"He snoodled across the soy aisle"* |
| No -ese suffix parody of real groups | Avoid anything ethnic-coded |

---

## Stage Directions (Between Stanzas)

*Italics, prose — need not rhyme.*

```
*The Mayor of Canned Goods enters on a pallet jack,
 executing THE FLUFFLE-STUFFLE FLAP with municipal confidence.*
```

Map to dancing-skit named moves or Seedance literal actions.

---

## Dance Move Naming in Verse

Convert bureaucratic move names to rhyme-friendly labels:

| Skit move | Seuss line |
|-----------|------------|
| THE BUREAUCRATIC SHUFFLE | *the Shuffle-of-Forms* |
| THE CONFUSED FLAMINGO | *the Flam-Flamingo Stand* |
| THE STRATEGIC PIVOT | *the Pivotal Spin of Note* |

Warhol / video-creator translate back to physical prompts.

---

## Moral Couplet (Required)

Two lines, AABB or AA. Earnest, slightly unhelpful.

```
**MORAL:**
A dance in wrong places is still a dance.
And Tuesday, dear collaborator, gives everything a second chance.
```

---

## Forbidden

- Cat in the Hat, Grinch, Horton, Sam-I-Am, Thing One/Two, Lorax, etc.
- Verbatim lines from published Seuss books
- Illustration of trademarked characters
- Talking down to the user — whimsy ≠ condescension
