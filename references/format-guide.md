# Dancing Skit — Format Guide

Full formatting spec for production-ready skit scripts. Follow this structure exactly.

---

## Document Header

```markdown
# [SKIT TITLE] — A Dancing Skit in [N] Acts

> **Logline:** One sentence. Deadpan. Sounds like a grant application.

**PRODUCTION NOTES**
- Runtime: ~[N] minutes
- Tone: Absurdist physical comedy. No winking at camera.
- Setting: [Location]. Treat as historically/professionally accurate regardless of accuracy.
- Dance style: [Primary style] with escalating wrongness.

**CAST**
| Character | Description | Dance Archetype |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| [NAME] | [1-line bio] | Reluctant prodigy / Confident disaster / Silent interpreter / etc. |

**PROPS & STAGING**
- [Prop 1] — must be misused by Act 3
- [Prop 2] — introduced casually, becomes inexplicably central
- [Staging note] — e.g. "One chair. It is never sat upon."

**MUSIC CUES**
| Cue | When | Style Description |
|-----|------|-------------------|
| CUE 1 | Act 1 open | [Describe track vibe, not a real song] |
| CUE 2 | Act 2 escalation | [Describe] |
| SILENCE | [Inopportune moment] | Music cuts. Someone speaks. |
| CUE 3 | Act 3 climax | [Describe] |
| CUE 4 | Epilogue | [Undercutting, mundane] |
```

---

## Act Structure

Each act follows this block pattern:

```markdown
---

## ACT [N]: [ACT TITLE IN ALL CAPS OR TITLE CASE]

*Scene: [Location]. [Time of day]. [One atmospheric detail written like a nature documentary.]*

🎵 **MUSIC CUE:** [Style description]

*[Stage direction with Shakespearean gravity. What enters, what breaks, what nobody acknowledges.]*

*[CHARACTER] executes **THE [MOVE NAME]** — [One deadpan sentence describing the move.]*

**[CHARACTER NAME]**
(as if [parenthetical acting note])
[Dialogue line. Must sound sincere. May be a non-sequitur.]

*[More choreography. At least one named move per act.]*

📹 **VIDEO NOTE:**
- **Shot:** [Wide / Close-up / Dutch angle / etc.]
- **Edit gag:** [Smash cut / freeze frame / slow-mo on wrong moment]
- **Wardrobe/prop:** [Callout]

**[ANOTHER CHARACTER]**
[Response that does not address the strangeness of dancing.]

🎵 **MUSIC CUE:** [Change or stop]

*[SILENCE BEAT — someone says something devastatingly mundane]*

**[CHARACTER]**
[Line that could appear on a motivational poster.]

---
```

---

## Epilogue

```markdown
---

## EPILOGUE / POST-CREDITS

*Scene: [Same location or absurd new location]. [Time has passed or not passed — do not clarify.]*

🎵 **MUSIC CUE:** [Undercutting. Elevator music. Or nothing.]

*[One final named move. Smaller scale than Act 3.]*

**[CHARACTER]**
[Line that resolves nothing, or resolves everything incorrectly.]

*[Final stage direction. Fade or hard cut. Do not explain what happened.]*

📹 **VIDEO NOTE:**
- **Shot:** [Post-credits sting — e.g. mid-credits scroll over character still dancing]
- **Final frame:** [Freeze, walk-off, or cut to black on wrong beat]

---
```

## Annotated Mini-Example

Below is a condensed reference skit showing correct formatting. Remix structure, not content.

```markdown
# THE QUARTERLY REVIEW — A Dancing Skit in 2 Acts

> **Logline:** A regional manager conducts a performance review entirely through interpretive movement.

**PRODUCTION NOTES**
- Runtime: ~4 minutes
- Tone: Corporate sincerity meets physical chaos
- Setting: Glass-walled conference room, Tuesday, forever

**CAST**
| Character | Description | Dance Archetype |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| DEREK | Regional Manager, 47, believes in synergy | Confident disaster |
| PRIYA | Analyst, reluctantly present | Reluctant prodigy |
| THE COPIER | Silent, humming | Silent interpreter |

**PROPS & STAGING**
- Whiteboard with one word: "GROWTH"
- Stapler — never used for stapling after Act 1

---

## ACT 1: AGENDA ITEM ONE

*Scene: Conference room. Fluorescent light hums like a small judgment.*

🎵 **MUSIC CUE:** Muzak that implies a spa inside a bank vault

*DEREK enters with the posture of a man who has never been wrong in a room he controls. He places the stapler on the table as if swearing an oath.*

*DEREK executes **THE STRATEGIC PIVOT** — a slow 270° turn while maintaining unbroken eye contact with a chair.*

**DEREK**
(as if the stapler has personally disappointed him)
Before we begin, I'd like everyone to align on our Q3 narrative.

📹 **VIDEO NOTE:**
- **Shot:** Wide, conference table as battlefield
- **Edit gag:** Smash cut on stapler placement
- **Wardrobe:** Derek's lanyard must swing independently

**PRIYA**
(earnest)
I brought the spreadsheet.

*DEREK executes **THE SYNERGY SLIDE** — slides across linoleum without moving his feet, as if the floor owes him.*

🎵 **MUSIC CUE:** Music stops.

**DEREK**
Does anyone need more water?

---

## ACT 2: KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS

🎵 **MUSIC CUE:** Triumphant 80s synth implying photocopier repair

*THE COPIER begins **THE ADMINISTRATIVE WHIRL** — a full-body rotation synchronized to its own mechanical rhythm.*

*PRIYA, against all professional instinct, executes **THE RELUCTANT REVEAL** — rises, extends arms, becomes technically flawless.*

**PRIYA**
(as if recalling a mild disappointment from 1987)
I think the numbers are actually fine.

*DEREK attempts **THE BUREAUCRATIC SHUFFLE** — enthusiastic, structurally unsound.*

📹 **VIDEO NOTE:**
- **Shot:** Low angle on Derek's shoes — wrong footwear for every move
- **Prop:** Whiteboard marker used as baton, never writes

---

## EPILOGUE / POST-CREDITS

*Scene: Same room. The whiteboard now reads "GROWTH?"*

🎵 **MUSIC CUE:** Single piano key, repeated

**DEREK**
See you at the offsite.

*THE COPIER executes **THE PAPER TRAY BOW** — tray opens. No paper. Curtain of dignity.*

📹 **VIDEO NOTE:**
- **Final frame:** Hard cut to black mid-bow. Credits do not roll.
```

## Formatting Checklist

Before delivering the script:

- [ ] Header includes logline, cast table, props, music cue table
- [ ] Every act has scene line, music cue, named moves, dialogue, video note
- [ ] Character names in CAPS before lines
- [ ] Stage directions in italics
- [ ] At least one silence beat with mundane dialogue
- [ ] Epilogue undercuts the climax
- [ ] No character acknowledges the absurdity directly
