# Scottish Accent Guide (Phonetic, Meme-Safe)

Write dialogue and captions so they **read** Scottish aloud. Strong accent, clear spelling. Affectionate, not mocking.

---

## Core Phonetic Patterns

| Standard | Phonetic hint | Example |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| not | nae / no | "That's nae a haggis, that's ma lunch" |
| don't | dinnae | "Dinnae drop the haggis" |
| can't | cannae | "Ye cannae fling a haggis indoors" |
| you | ye / yeh | "Ye ken what I mean?" |
| your | yer | "Yer kilt's on back tae front" |
| the | th' / tha | "Th' bagpipes are loadin'" |
| my | ma | "Ma haggis, ma rules" |
| little | wee | "A wee spin, aye" |
| know | ken | "Ye ken th' drill" |
| about | aboot | "Aboot time we danced" |
| out | oot | "Oot th' way, haggis comin' through" |
| night | nicht | "Dance a' nicht" |
| right | richt | "Richt then, fling it" |
| what | whit | "Whit in th' Highlands is that?" |
| children | weans / bairns | "Th' weans are learnin' th' fling" |
| very | pure / dead | "Pure braw moves" |
| yes | aye | "Aye, aye, aye!" |
| oh | och | "Och, here we go again" |

---

## Power Words (sprinkle liberally)

`och`, `aye`, `nae`, `wee`, `bonnie`, `braw`, `ken`, `dinnae`, `laddie`, `lassie`, `haud`, `gie`, `tae`, `frae`, `eh`

---

## Sentence Rhythm

- Drop trailing g: dancin', flingin', tossin'
- Contract: it's → it's, I am → Ah'm, going to → gonnae
- Rhetorical **eh** at end: "Grand fling, eh?"
- **Och** opens 1 in 3 lines

## Hard Avoid

- Slurs or punching down at Scottish identity
- "Groundskeeper Willie" as only reference — create original characters (Hamish, Morag, etc.)
- Incomprehensible over-phoneticization — must stay readable
